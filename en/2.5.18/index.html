Statement

$2.5.18^*.$ A particle of mass $m_1$ flew at a velocity $v$ on a stationary particle of mass $m_2$, which, after an elastic impact, flew at an angle $\alpha$ to the initial direction of movement of the incoming particle. Determine the velocity of a particle of mass $m_2$ after impact.

Solution

This phenomenon is also knowned as Compton scattering

Let's write the law of conservation of momentum: $$\left\{\begin{matrix} X\text{: }m_1v=m_1v_1\cos\beta+m_2v_2\cos\alpha \\ Y\text{: }m_1v_1\sin\beta=m_2v_2\sin\alpha \\ \end{matrix}\right.$$ Let's $$\kappa=\frac{m_2}{m_1}$$

From where: $$v_1\sin\beta=\kappa v_2\sin\alpha$$ $$v=v_1\cos\beta+\kappa v_2\cos\alpha$$ We could find $\cos\beta$ from first expression: $$\cos\beta=\sqrt{1-\sin^2\beta}$$ $$\cos\beta=\sqrt{1-(\kappa \frac{v_2}{v_1}\sin\alpha)^2}$$ The law of conservation of energy: $$\frac{m_1v^2}{2}=\frac{m_1v_1^2}{2}+\frac{m_2v_2^2}{2}$$ From where $$v_1^2=v^2-\kappa v_2^2$$ From the other hand: $$(v-\kappa v_2\cos\alpha)^2=v_1^2\cos^2\beta$$ $$(v-\kappa v_2\cos\alpha)^2=v_1^2\cdot(1-(\kappa \frac{v_2}{v_1}\sin\alpha)^2)$$ $$(v-\kappa v_2\cos\alpha)^2=v_1^2-(\kappa v_2\sin\alpha)^2$$ $$v^2 - 2\kappa vv_2\cos\alpha=v_1^2-\kappa^2 v_2^2$$ From where $$v_2^2\kappa(1+\kappa)=2\kappa vv_2\cos\alpha$$ $$v_2=v\frac{2\cos\alpha}{1+\kappa}$$ And, finally $$\boxed{v_2=v\frac{2m_1\cos\alpha}{m_1+m_2}}$$

Answer

$$v_2=v\frac{2m_1\cos\alpha}{m_1+m_2}$$
