Statement

$2.8.37^*.$ A wheel of radius $R$ can rotate freely around its axis. Drive belts moving at a speed $v$ are pressed against the side surface of the wheel at a distance $h$ from the axis of rotation. Determine the steady-state angular velocity of the wheel if its contact with the drive belt occurs only along the rim.

Solution

From the geometry of the problem $$\left\{\begin{matrix} \frac{h}{R} = \cos\alpha \\ \frac{u}{v} = \cos\alpha \end{matrix}\right.$$ Considering the relationship between velocity $u$ and angular velocity $\omega$ $$\left\{\begin{matrix} \frac{h}{R} = \frac{u}{v} \\ u=\omega R \end{matrix}\right.$$ From where $$\boxed{\omega=\frac{hv}{R^2}}$$

Answer

$$\omega=\frac{hv}{R^2}$$
